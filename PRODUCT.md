# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

adaptive

## Users

People worldwide deciding what to do in the next two hours: commuters, walkers, riders, parents, event-goers, athletes, and anyone who needs a direct answer about rain at their current or saved location. They usually open the app quickly, outdoors or while preparing to leave, and need the answer before detail.

## Product Purpose

Weathercast is a rain-only nowcasting app. It answers whether rain will reach a location in the next 120 minutes, when it should begin, how intense it may become, when it should end, and how confident the system is. Success means useful, well-calibrated decisions with fewer false alarms and lower rain-onset error than general weather apps, measured honestly by location and data tier.

## Positioning

The clearest, most trustworthy answer to one question: exactly when will it rain here?

## Brand Personality

Precise, calm, and candid. Weathercast sounds like an expert sitting beside the user: direct about the likely outcome, transparent about uncertainty, never dramatic.

## Anti-references

Do not resemble dense professional meteorology consoles, generic weather dashboards filled with unrelated metrics, gamified forecast apps, or interfaces that imply street-level certainty unsupported by source data. Avoid decorative glass, fake radar, alarming color everywhere, and vague percentages without a decision.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the decision, then evidence.
2. Express uncertainty as useful ranges and plain confidence, never false precision.
3. Keep rain timing central; every secondary feature must support it.
4. Adapt to native iOS and Android behavior while preserving one recognizable product.
5. Earn accuracy claims through archived verification, data provenance, and regional quality labels.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA-equivalent mobile behavior. Support Dynamic Type and Android font scaling, screen readers, 44 pt iOS and 48 dp Android touch targets, high contrast, reduced motion, dark mode, non-color state cues, plain-language uncertainty, and locale-aware time formatting.
